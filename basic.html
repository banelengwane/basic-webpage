These are my historic places

District Six

On the 11 Feb 1966, District Six was declared a White area under the 1950 Group Areas Act of the Apartheid government In 1970 District six was renamed Zonnebloem, a name that makes reference to an 18th century colonial farm The district was established as a community of freed slaves, merchants, artisans, labourers and immigrants

City Hall

The City Hall is a large Edwardian building in Cape Town city centre which was built in 1905. It is located on the Grand Parade to the west of the Castle and is built from honey-coloured oolitic limestone imported from Bath in England. On 11 Feb, 1990. The late President, Mr Nelson Mandela made his first public speech after his release from the balcony of Cape Town City Hall.

Iziko Museum

In 1897 the Museum moved to its present building in the historic Company's Garden The Museum was founded in 1825, was known as the South African Museum, the first in the country The museum houses more than one and a half million specimens of scientific importance, including 700 million year old fossils
